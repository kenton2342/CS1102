Other Mechanisms



Delegated Proof-of-Stake (DPoS) allows token holders to vote for a limited number of trusted delegates who validate transactions on their behalf. This approach improves transaction speed and efficiency. However, it risks being more centralized, as power becomes concentrated among a few elected delegates, potentially undermining the democratic nature of the network.



Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (PBFT), commonly employed in permissioned blockchain, operates through iterative voting among a known set of validators and can tolerate up to one-third malicious actors. It is ideal for enterprise environments that prioritize high reliability. Its main drawbacks include limited scalability and communication overhead, which make it less suitable for large, open networks.
